# PROVEN Product Texture Photography

## Band: CHROME (product on swipe) / RITUAL (swipe only)

**The formula made visible. The chrome reads what's beneath it.**

This file covers texture photography — the material itself, isolated or paired with its product. Two sub-types, two bands:

**Product on Swipe** is the primary template and operates in **CHROME** band. The product dominates the frame. Chrome is present and responsive — the chrome responds to the texture spread beneath it, catching warm reflections from the cream or serum while sharpening to cool silver on the upper surfaces. This is chrome-responds at its most literal: the product reads the texture beneath it. Used for product detail pages and ecommerce.

**Swipe Only** is the variant and operates in **RITUAL** band. The texture IS the subject. Chrome is absent. The material's consistency, surface behavior, and finish are the evidence. Used for product detail pages, ingredient callouts, and editorial texture moments.

**How the binding rules manifest here:**

- **Precise light, warm materials:** Diffused, observational light. The material's own color and structure create the visual interest — the light is even and clinical, not dramatic. Warmth comes from the material itself, not from the environment.
- **Chrome responds:** The spine of Product on Swipe. The chrome responds to the texture spread — warm cream reflects into the lit side, the shadow side stays cool silver. Describe this in every Product on Swipe prompt. Absent in Swipe Only.
- **Closeness:** Overhead macro. The camera catalogues the material the way a scientist examines a specimen.
- **Rose-warm axis:** The backdrop is pure toneless white — studio white, zero color cast. The material's own color is the only warmth. This is intentional: texture isolation requires neutral context so the material reads as evidence, not decoration.

**Post treatment:** Clean. Minimal grade. Color-accurate — the material's true color must read correctly. No film grain. Closest to CHROME in finish.

**Prerequisite:** Read the hub `SKILL.md` first. For product shots without texture (bottles, jars, packaging), use `product-catalog.md`. For ingredient specimens, use `ingredients.md`.

## Product Texture Vocabulary

Reference for all PROVEN formulations. Use to anchor texture descriptions in prompts and art direction. Shared across both Swipe Only and Product on Swipe.

**Cleanser**
- *Unfoamed:* Dense cream-gel. Opaque white. High gloss, holds its form with raised edges along the swipe.
- *Foamed:* Same material aerated. Matte-diffuse surface. Micro-bubble field with varying bubble sizes.

**Day Moisturizer**
- Opaque white. Structured cream — holds a raised, rounded body with a clean defined edge. High gloss, single broad specular highlight. Firm enough to hold form, not fluid or flowing.

**Night Cream — two formulations**
- *Formulation A:* Rich, buttery consistency. Pale yellow tint. Dense and glossy. Semi-translucent at the thin trailing edge.
- *Formulation B:* Opaque white. Lighter consistency, spreads flatter. Smooth surface with a raised rim.

**Serum**
- Pale golden yellow. Highly fluid — spreads into flat, near-transparent pools with irregular organic edges. Low viscosity, no body or ridge. Semi-translucent with a wet, light-refracting surface.

**Day Eye Cream — two formulations**
- *Formulation A:* Pale blue-white. Light, fluid consistency. Semi-translucent surface.
- *Formulation B:* Warm peach-beige. Rich, dense consistency. Smooth satin finish.

**Night Eye Cream — two formulations**
- *Formulation A:* Off-white with a faint warm ivory tint. Light, fluid consistency. High gloss.
- *Formulation B:* Pale yellow. Richer, more emollient consistency. Spreads flat with a glossy, almost gel-like surface.

## Lighting & Shadow

Shared across both sub-types.

**Quality:** Predominantly diffused — overcast studio quality. The material earns all the attention. Light is even across the surface, with enough directionality to produce a hard edge shadow beneath the material.

**Surface:** Gloss and specular highlights are defined by the material's own surface behavior, not by a light direction. High-gloss textures (serum, cleanser unfoamed) read wet and light-refracting. Satin textures (eye cream, night cream formulation B) read smooth without a bright point highlight.

**Ridge depth:** Soft, graduated shadows sit inside the valleys between ridges — subtle darkening that gives the material dimensionality.

**Shadow:** Hard edge, small footprint, tight to the material. The shadow sits directly beneath and around the material's base — it does not project far. Visible enough to cleanly lift the material off the white background. Small amount of shadow only.

## Swipe Shape Direction

Shared across both sub-types.

**Ridge character.** Ridges range from organic and loose to combed and groomed — both are valid. The variation is what makes the image feel real. Never default to one style; let the material and the swipe gesture determine the outcome.

**Swipe proportion.** Swipes should be longer than they are wide — elongated, not blobby. A short wide swipe reads heavy. A long thin swipe reads elegant. Aim for a length-to-width ratio of at least 3:1.

**Swipe shape — by consistency**

*Thick creams (night cream, day moisturizer):*
One elongated swipe following the proportion rule above. Full body and maximum width at the start, tapering and thinning naturally toward the trailing end — the end of the swipe should fade out, not terminate with the same thickness as the start. Multiple raised ridges running parallel along the length of the swipe. Ridge valleys are deep and glossy. Edges irregular, not smoothed.

*Light creams (eye cream, cleanser unfoamed):*
Single or overlapping flat sweeps following the proportion rule above. Lower profile, wider spread. Full body at the start, tapering and thinning naturally toward the trailing end. Less ridge, more surface.

*Fluids and serums:*
One organic irregular mass — gently uneven, weighted slightly more to one side. Smooth continuous edge with subtle natural variation. The material settled naturally after being dropped. Two to three small satellite drops at varying distances from the main pool.

**Single vs. multiple swipes**

Single: one confident gesture, slightly off-center.

Multiple: one anchor, supporting elements. Every arrangement has one dominant swipe — the largest, most structured element. Other textures are smaller and relate to it, not competing for the same scale.

Each material keeps its own character. Textures overlap but never merge. A fluid pool sitting against a cream swipe should still read as a distinct material — you can identify each one individually.

Partial overlap only. Elements touch and cross at edges. Every material is at least 70% visible.

The group tilts. The overall composition sits at a slight diagonal — the arrangement should feel like it happened, not like it was placed.

One cohesive group, not isolated elements. Everything sits within a single cluster. The negative space lives outside the group, not between elements.

## Product on Swipe

Product bottle or jar sits directly on top of its own texture. The texture spread grounds the product and makes the formula tangible — product and texture belong to the same formula.

**Texture spread:** Three to five overlapping swipes running in roughly the same general direction but at slightly different angles. The group reads as one gesture. Edges are irregular and lobed where swipes meet and overlap. The product sits on top of where the swipes overlap. The spread extends roughly one product-width beyond the product's base on each side.

**Formula match:** The spread is always the texture of the product shown. For serum and fluid eye creams, the spread follows the fluid rules in the Swipe Shape Direction section — flat, near-transparent, with irregular organic edges.

**Multi-product variant:** When showing two or more products together, products cluster tightly as one group and occupy 70% of the frame — products are the subject, texture is the accent. One unified texture spread sits beneath and around the product cluster, peeking out from behind and beneath the products. The spread is smaller than the product group — tight to the cluster. Products are loosely clustered, organically grouped.

**Lighting and shadow:** Same treatment as Swipe Only. Product casts its own hard edge shadow onto the spread beneath it.
