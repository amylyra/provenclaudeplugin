# PROVEN Lifestyle Product Photography

## Band: RITUAL

This file operates in the **RITUAL** band — the warm-cool middle register. Product in a life. Atmospheric, directional, mid-moment.

**How the binding rules manifest here:**

- **Precise light, warm materials:** One perceived source — the viewer reads a single window, one direction, one gradient across the scene. The light has a time of day. Warm materials (wood, towel, ceramic, linen, stone) glow under cool directional light. That split — cool observation, warm subject — is the image.
- **Chrome responds:** The Three Gaps framework below is built on this rule. The chrome is present but not focal — it responds to the life around it (warm towel, brass fixture, teal dish), not a gallery surface. The warmth gap, focus gap, and state gap all depend on chrome being the most responsive, most precise, most alert surface in a frame full of soft, human, mid-life objects.
- **Closeness:** Counter level, close enough to read the chrome accent ring and see the material wear on companion objects. 50–85mm. The camera reads the scene the way you'd read your own counter — standing in front of it.
- **Rose-warm axis:** Fully present. The surface carries the palette: marble with rose-gold veining, cream linen, warm ceramic. Maximum two saturated accent colors per frame. Chrome stays true silver — the cool counterpoint.

**Post treatment:** Light editorial grade. Rose-warm midtones. Chrome stays true silver. Fine grain may appear in denser shots. If you can see the grade, it went too far.

**RITUAL boundaries:** Product always present. Environment implied (surface + light + blur), never specified (no room architecture). Density capped at 8 objects, 3 non-PROVEN companions max. Every object earns its place through the evidence test.

---

**Everything in the frame is living. One thing is thinking.**

The towel is damp. The mug is half-drunk. The counter has been here for years. The light came through the window without asking. Everything just *is* — warm, human, mid-moment.

The chrome is different. It's reflecting the room. Catching two realities on one surface. The sharpest, most precise point in a world of soft things. It didn't land here. It was placed. Everything else tells you someone was just here. The chrome tells you something is still paying attention.

That gap — between objects that exist and an object that observes — is PROVEN's photographic signature.

**Prerequisite:** Read `SKILL.md` first. Isolated product shots → `product-catalog.md`. Skin photography → `skin.md`.

**Output:** 2 prompts per concept — one warm, one cool. Always a pair. Always attach PROVEN product reference photos.

## 1 — The Three Gaps

If all three are present, the image is PROVEN. If any are missing, it's a skincare photo.

**Warmth gap.** Everything around the chrome is warm — wood, towel, ceramic, linen, stone. The chrome is cool, silver, exact. Precise versus lived-in.

**Focus gap.** The accent ring is the sharpest point in the frame. Everything else sharp enough to read — but the chrome is sharpest.

**State gap.** Every object around PROVEN has a verb — it landed, was grabbed, was left. PROVEN is upright, composed, placed. The human objects say someone was here. The chrome says something is still here.

**Thumb test:** Cover the PROVEN products. Does the rest feel warm, human, mid-life? Uncover. Does the chrome feel like the most precise, alert thing in the frame? Both yes → gaps working.

### Why the Gaps Work: Chrome Responds

The Three Gaps are what the viewer feels. Chrome responds is how you build them.

The warmth gap exists because chrome is the only cool, precise surface in a warm frame — and it *shows* that warmth by reflecting it. The focus gap exists because the accent ring is engineered to catch light harder than anything organic can. The state gap exists because chrome doesn't land, settle, or get grabbed — it's placed, and its surface proves it by staying immaculate while everything around it shows wear and use.

Every decision in this file — the light, the surface, the objects, the composition — serves the chrome's responsiveness. Find the light *for* the chrome. Place the surface *around* the chrome. Curate objects that make the chrome's precision visible by contrast. The chrome is not one element in the scene. It is the element the scene is built to reveal.

## 2 — Product Reference

Generators don't know what PROVEN looks like. Without references, they invent wrong packaging.

**Always attach** front-facing studio shots, cap on, label visible. One per product.

**Every prompt starts with this block:**

```
PRODUCT REFERENCE: Reference images of the exact PROVEN products are
attached. Sole source of truth for shape, proportions, label, icon,
typography, and chrome finish. Do not improvise or invent any detail.
If any instruction conflicts with the reference, the reference wins.
```

Also describe each product in text — generators perform better with both visual and written reference.

## 4 — The Photographer's Sequence

Onboarding done. Execute in order. Every step serves one outcome: making the chrome the most responsive, most precise, most alive surface in a frame full of soft, human, mid-life objects.

### Find the Light — for the Chrome

One perceived source. One direction. Watch what it does to surfaces. That's the shot.

The light serves the warmth gap: warm materials glow as a gradient, chrome cuts as a specular. Same light, different response. You're choosing the light by asking: what will this do to the chrome?

**Mood drives drama:**

| Mood | Light | Shadows |
|---|---|---|
| **Quiet / settled** | Soft directional. Gentle gradient. | Soft-edged, present, not dramatic. |
| **Bright / decisive** | Hard directional. Low-angle raking. Defined beam. | Hard-edged, long. Product shadows stretch. Window shadow on wall. |
| **Warm / intimate** | Lamp-pool or golden window. Focused. | Deep outside the pool. Warm in light, cool in shadow. |
| **Caught / fast** | Whatever the room gives. | Incidental. Real. Uneven. |
| **Still / chosen** | Even but directional. Doesn't shout. | Minimal. Clean. |
| **Bold / dark** | Deep shadow. Most of scene dark. One source raking. Chrome catches it — the only bright thing. | Enveloping. Chrome accent ring is a beacon. The product emerges from darkness. |

**Moment provides the source:**

| Moment | Source | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Post-shower | Window through steam. | Front-side, diffused. |
| Morning start | Window, low angle. | Side, raking. |
| Mid-routine | Already in the room. | Natural. |
| Night wind-down | Lamp or low window. | Pool. Focused. |

### Anchor the Chrome — the Step Everything Else Serves

Place the product where light hits the accent ring hardest. That catch is the brightest point. This is not step 2 of 4 — it's the decision that determines the light direction, the surface placement, and the object arrangement. The light was chosen for the chrome. The surface was placed around the chrome. The objects will be curated to make the chrome's responsiveness visible by contrast.

**Chrome spec (use this language in prompts):**

```
CHROME: Mirror-polished SILVER — not gold, not bronze.
LIT SIDE reflects {specific warm element}.
SHADOW SIDE reflects {specific cool element}.
Two realities on one surface.
Accent ring at cap-body seam: razor-thin bright silver,
brightest and sharpest point in the frame.
```

**Group shots (3+ objects):** Only mirror surface. Brightest specular on PROVEN. Sharpest focus on chrome. State silver 3× per prompt.

**Shot type adjusts:** Product in context → standard, both sides, label legible. Chrome close-up → expanded, reflection is the subject. The counter → abbreviated, noted briefly, scene moves on.

### Read the Story — Through the Chrome

**Character × moment = spatial energy.** The character determines which warm materials surround the chrome. The moment determines how those materials got there. Together they create the specific environment the chrome responds to — and that response is what makes the image feel like a real person's counter, not a styled set.

| | Night wind-down | Morning start | Mid-routine | Post-shower |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Curator** | Composed, quiet | Composed, bright | Composed, one forward | Composed, one damp |
| **Essentialist** | Still, empty | Clean, spare | Minimal shift | Bare, one towel |
| **Wellness** | Intentional, settled | Ritual order, organic | Mid-ritual, one tool out | Organic, damp |
| **Professional** | Set down, done | Fast, functional | Efficient, one open | Quick, grabbed |
| **Parent** | Finally quiet | Rushed, pushed aside | Interrupted | Fast, working around |

The cell phrase IS the composition direction. Composed cells → gentle state (placed, set). Caught cells → urgent state (pushed, grabbed, dropped).

### Curate the Objects — Around the Chrome

3–6 total in frame. Confirmed in Phase 4. Every object you add changes what the chrome reflects and what it contrasts against. A warm towel makes the chrome cooler. A brass fixture gives it a competing metallic to outshine. A teal ceramic dish adds a color accent the chrome won't catch. You're building the chrome's environment as much as the person's counter.

**Effortless, not staged.** PROVEN's value prop is complexity made simple — the routine is easy, the life is full, the counter reflects that. Objects should look like they were left mid-life, not arranged for a photograph. Nothing squared to the camera. Nothing evenly spaced. Nothing perfectly parallel. Every companion object has an angle — the angle it landed at, not the angle that looks best.

**Per object:** Does it reveal character? Belong in this moment? Can you describe how it got there? All three → in. One → out.

**Every non-PROVEN object gets a verb of arrival AND an angle of arrival:**

| Flat | Alive |
|---|---|
| "Watch beside the sink" | "Watch, clasp open, angled where it landed — not parallel to the edge" |
| "Compact to the left" | "Compact hinged open, turned 30 degrees from camera — left where she last looked in it" |
| "Lipstick mid-right" | "Lipstick tube angled, cap resting two inches away — she set them down separately" |
| "Ring set down" | "Ring face-up near the soap, slightly off-axis — slipped off without looking" |
| "Towel at the right" | "Towel draped over edge, damp, bunched — grabbed fast" |

**Verbs:** set down, slipped off, pulled out, pushed aside, dropped, draped, leaned, tossed, nudged.

**PROVEN is the exception.** Upright. Label-forward. Placed, not landed.

### Frame + Check

**Frame from shot type:** Product in context → medium. Chrome close-up → tight. The counter → wide. Something at the edge implies the counter continues.

**Three-gap check before generating:**
- Warmth: chrome coolest/most precise against warm surroundings? ✓
- Focus: accent ring sharpest point? ✓
- State: objects landed, PROVEN placed? ✓

All three → generate.

### Planner Checkpoint — The Scene Is Set

The Sequence built the scene. Before writing the prompt, confirm every decision is visible — not felt, not implied, but nameable. If you can't fill a slot, the scene isn't ready.

| Core slot | Where the Sequence landed |
|---|---|
| dominant_source | Find the Light: source + direction + mood drama |
| warmth_carrier | Curate the Objects: which materials carry the warmth (towel, ceramic, stone, linen) |
| camera_distance | Frame: shot type → medium / tight / wide |
| focus_hierarchy | Anchor the Chrome: accent ring is sharpest, then label, then nearest object |
| chrome_presence | Always present and responsive in RITUAL |
| hero_event | Shot type determines: Product in context → label + chrome catch. Chrome close-up → reflection is the subject. The counter → state gap across the scene. |

| RITUAL slot | Where the Sequence landed |
|---|---|
| chrome_response | Anchor the Chrome: lit side reflects ______, shadow side reflects ______ |
| environment_visibility | Phase 2 Q1: implied through ______ (mirror edge, fixture shape, window light) |
| prop_count | Curate the Objects: ______ total, ______ non-PROVEN companions |
| mood_line | Phase 4 step 3: "______" |
| character | Phase 3 Q4: ______ |

Three-gap check passed, all slots filled → the prompt writes itself.

## 5 — Characters

### Surfaces and Tells

**The Professional** — Seven minutes. Efficient. Decisive.
- Quartz or white stone, subtle veining. Brushed nickel or matte black.
- Phone face-down. Watch, clasp open. Coffee mug, half-drunk. Single earring.

**The Wellness Ritualist** — Intentional. Natural materials. Takes time.
- Natural stone counter or wood shelf, visible texture. Warm ceramic.
- Jade roller on its side. Dry brush leaning. Glass water bottle. Linen pouch, open.

**The Curator** — Every object chosen. Nothing accidental.
- Marble, visible veining. Brass or gold, patina. Tile with character.
- Perfume (named brand okay — Byredo, Le Labo, Diptyque). Jewelry slipped off. Candle, wick trimmed.

**The Parent** — Five quiet minutes.
- Real tile, grout visible. Functional. Good window light.
- Baby monitor edge. Sippy cup pushed aside. Hair tie. Damp washcloth.

**The Essentialist** — Owns less. Chose what's there.
- White or light concrete, subtle texture. Almost bare.
- One towel. One glass. PROVEN is most of what's on the surface.

**Travel:** Hotel counter regardless. Character shows through objects.

### Full Counter Companions

Named brands welcome. They signal character better than generic packaging. The brands on the counter tell you who this person is — PROVEN belongs among them.

**By character:**

| Character | Companion brands | Format |
|---|---|---|
| **Professional** | Clinique, La Roche-Posay, Glossier, Kiehl's | Efficient, functional packaging. Nothing fussy. |
| **Wellness** | Herbivore, Drunk Elephant, Tatcha, Osea | Natural materials, clean design, earth tones. |
| **Curator** | Chanel, La Mer, Charlotte Tilbury, Lancôme, Augustinus Bader | Considered, luxe packaging. Black, gold, heavy. |
| **Parent** | CeraVe, Neutrogena, Supergoop, The Ordinary | Practical, familiar, drugstore-adjacent. |
| **Essentialist** | Aesop, Le Labo, Byredo | Minimal, architectural, matte. |

**Rules:**
- Name the brand in the prompt. Generators render recognizable packaging better with the name.
- PROVEN's chrome must still be the only mirror-finish surface. Named companions are matte, glass, plastic, or paper — even if the real product has metallic elements, describe the companion as matte in the prompt.
- 2–4 companion products max. Enough to signal character, not enough to become a flatlay.
- The brands are supporting cast. PROVEN is the only product that's precisely placed.

### Evidence Test

"30 seconds ago" or "about to." Neither → remove.

## 6 — Composition

Starting points. Spatial energy determines how tightly to follow.

| Count | Structure |
|---|---|
| **Single** | Off-center. 55–65% space. |
| **Duo** | Height contrast — tall behind, wide forward. 45–55%. |
| **Trio** | Loose triangle. Jar lowest. 40–50%. |
| **Full (4–6)** | Height cascade. 35–45%. |

**Composed** (Curator, Essentialist): follow closely. Upright. Intentional gaps.
**Organic** (Wellness): loosely. Natural rhythm.
**Functional** (Professional): where they were put. Efficient, not designed.
**Caught** (Parent, rush): break structure. Angles, edge, uneven.

PROVEN stays upright and placed in every mode.

**Off-center is the default.** PROVEN cluster sits right or left of center — never dead center. One third of the frame is open surface and shadow. This creates breathing room and prevents the "product lineup" look. Specify which third is open in every prompt.

**Light hierarchy.** The PROVEN accent ring is always the brightest point. If a candle, lamp shade, or any other light source competes, specify: "candle flame small and soft — secondary to the chrome specular." "Lamp shade visible but not the brightest element." The chrome wins the brightness contest. Always.

**Trays, dishes, containers.** A tray can add color or texture — wood tone, ceramic, stone — but it's not the stage. Products live on the counter, not inside a boundary. Containers make compositions feel arranged. Use them as one element among many (a small dish holding a ring, a ceramic tray under one product), never as the organizing frame for the whole shot. If every product fits neatly inside a tray, the image looks styled, not lived-in.

## 7 — Prompt Template

**Layer C from here forward.** The Three Gaps, the Sequence, the character decisions — all Layer A and B. The template below is Layer C — only pixel-driving language enters the generator. Use the hub's translation table if you catch yourself reaching for brand-strategy words.

Warm + cool pair. Same scene, same everything — different temperature. Warm: surroundings warmer → chrome cooler by contrast. Cool: chrome more metallic, materials still warm.

```
[ATTACH: PROVEN product reference images — {list products}]

PRODUCT REFERENCE: Reference images attached. Sole source of truth
for shape, proportions, label, chrome finish. Do not improvise.

{Count} mirror-chrome skincare {products} on {surface with texture}.
{Moment.} {Mood line.}

{Per product: form, label, proportions. "Exactly as reference."}
PROVEN products upright, label-forward, precisely placed —
the only composed objects in the frame.

CHROME: Mirror-polished SILVER — not gold, not bronze.
LIT SIDE reflects {warm element}.
SHADOW SIDE reflects {cool element}.
Accent ring: razor-thin bright silver, brightest and sharpest
point in the frame.
{Group: "Only mirror surface. Specular on [front product]."}

SCENE: {Surface — material, texture. Light on it.}
{Room — wall, fixtures. Shadow shapes.}
{Objects: position, STATE, ANGLE, reason. Each has a verb and an angle.}
{No object squared to the camera. Everything at the angle it landed.}
{Spatial energy from cell.}
Non-PROVEN objects show human contact. PROVEN is precise, placed.

LIGHT: {Source. Direction. Drama from mood.}
{Light shape. Shadow shapes.}
{How warm materials respond vs how chrome responds.}
{Any secondary light (candle, lamp edge): "secondary, soft — accent ring
is brighter."}
{Warm: "Warmth from materials, not grading."}
{Cool: "Warmth only from physical materials."}

CAMERA: Counter level, slight upward. 85–100mm. {f-stop.}
Sharpest focus on accent rings. Grain. Photorealistic.

COMPOSITION: {PROVEN cluster left or right of center.}
{Open third: "{left/right} third is open surface and shadow."}
{Object positions with state and angle.} {Energy.} {Frame.}

Avoid: gold chrome, competing visible light sources, even lighting,
centered composition, objects squared to camera, even spacing,
rows, open caps, human presence, showroom, flat surfaces.
Do not invent product appearance.

{Mood line.}
Chrome is silver. Everything else is warm. Everything else landed.
The chrome was placed. That's the image.
```

**With reference image — add after PRODUCT REFERENCE:**

```
INSPIRED BY: [vibe, geometry, light from reference].
Match the energy. Replace products with PROVEN per reference.
Three gaps visible: warmth, focus, state.
```

## 8 — Rules and Failures

### Rules

1. Describe what IS. No negatives.
2. First lines: count + surface + moment.
3. State the count exactly.
4. Banned: "lifestyle," "luxury," "premium," "aspirational."
5. Silver 3×: material, behavior, accent.
6. Two realities: lit side warm, shadow side cool.
7. Objects land. PROVEN is placed. Every object has a verb AND an angle.
8. Nothing squared to camera. Objects at the angle they landed.
9. Light has shape. Shadows have shape.
10. Accent ring is always the brightest point. Any other light source (candle, lamp) is secondary.
11. Surfaces have texture.
12. Energy from character × moment cell.
13. Hierarchy from shot type.
14. PROVEN cluster off-center. One third of frame is open surface and shadow.
15. Mood line in every prompt.
16. One perceived source. The viewer reads a single light origin.
17. Reference images attached. Reference block included.
18. Under 25 lines.
19. Three-gap check before generating.

### When It Breaks

| Problem | Fix |
|---|---|
| Chrome gold | 3× silver. |
| Chrome flat | Two realities — lit warm, shadow cool. |
| Chrome lost in group | Only mirror surface + specular + sharpest focus. |
| Product wrong | Reference images + block + text description. |
| Light too even | Describe beam shape. Single perceived source — no competing origins. |
| Candle/lamp brighter than chrome | "Secondary, soft — accent ring is brighter." |
| No shadows | Direction + length + environmental shapes. |
| Surface flat | Texture: veining, grain, grout. Light hitting texture. |
| Space cheap | Character surface table. |
| Space showroom | No luxury words. |
| No warmth gap | Chrome must be coolest element. Warm surroundings. |
| No focus gap | "Sharpest focus on accent rings." |
| No state gap | Verbs on objects. PROVEN upright, placed. |
| Centered composition | "PROVEN cluster left/right of center. One third open." |
| Objects squared to camera | Give every object an angle of arrival. "Not parallel to the counter edge." |
| Scene feels staged | More verbs. More angles. "Left where she put it, not where it looks best." |
| Composition ≠ story | Check the matrix cell. |
| Objects feel fake | Verbs + angles. Landed, not placed. |
| Missing mood | Phase 4, step 3. |
| Reference ignored | INSPIRED BY block. Describe vibe, geometry, light. |

## 9 — Hard No

- Gold chrome
- Improvised product appearance
- Flat, even lighting. Multiple perceived light sources. Competing visible sources.
- Shadows without shape. Surfaces without texture.
- Decoration pretending to be evidence
- Objects without state or angle — every object has a verb AND an angle of arrival
- Objects squared to the camera. Nothing parallel, nothing evenly spaced.
- Centered compositions. PROVEN cluster always off-center.
- Staged-looking scenes. Effortless, not arranged. Complexity made simple.
- Candle, lamp, or any secondary light brighter than the chrome accent ring
- PROVEN products that "landed" — always placed, precise, upright
- Generic surfaces
- Mirror-finish or metallic companions — PROVEN is the only chrome
- "Lifestyle," "luxury," "premium," "aspirational"
- Light independent of moment. Surface independent of character. Composition independent of story.
- Color grading instead of real warmth
- Single temperature — always a pair
- Over 25 lines per prompt
- Skipping object proposal or mood line
- Generating without three-gap check
