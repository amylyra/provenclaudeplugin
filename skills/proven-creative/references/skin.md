# PROVEN Creative — Skin Photography

## Band: SKIN

This file operates in the **SKIN** band — the warmest, most cinematic register in the system. The signature candidate. Human photography at reading distance, with light that has opinion.

**How the binding rules manifest here:**

- **Precise light, warm materials:** The light is cinematic and directional — it rakes across a face, catches an arm, puts half the body in shadow. Strong single perceived source. High drama. But the light itself stays cool; the warmth comes from the skin and the environmental materials around it. The results feel warm because the body carries the warmth. 5000K+ color temperature. The light creates the emotional moment.
- **Chrome responds → The gaze responds:** Chrome is absent or peripheral in SKIN. The binding rule transfers: the camera's gaze becomes the responsive element, adapting to each person the way chrome adapts to each environment. The camera notices something specific about THIS person — the way light falls on this particular skin, the way this body moves through this particular morning. Specificity, not affirmation.
- **Closeness:** The tightest in the system. Skin close-ups at 4–6 inches from the surface. Pores visible, texture legible, micro-expressions caught. Reading distance, not flattering distance.
- **Rose-warm axis:** Governs the backdrop and grade direction — not the skin itself. The cream (#FAF6F2) backdrop surrounds every skin tone with warmth. Skin tones are protected by the tolerance table in the Color Contract below. The rose-warm world surrounds every skin tone; it never overrides one.

**Post treatment:** Cinematic. Rose-warm highlights, deep cool shadows. Contrast pushed. Film grain present. Desaturated orange. The grade is a storytelling tool, but skin-tone integrity is maintained across all skin tones — the grade is correction, not imposition. Most latitude in the system, but it never looks like a preset.

## Activated Terrain

This is the primitive. Skin only counts as PROVEN when a real micro-expression creates real folds, real creases, real muscular engagement in the surface. Without it, you have dermatology. With it, you have a portrait of a living system the algorithm can read.

Activated Terrain is to PROVEN what sweat-and-tension is to Nike: the signature behavior that makes every image recognizable before you see a logo.

**Prerequisite:** Read the hub `SKILL.md` first.

## The Feeling

The camera is not assessing. It is **noticing** — the way you notice the light catching someone's cheek across a table. Close, but not invasive. Scientific, but not clinical. The emotional register is **curiosity held inside quiet intimacy**. The visual register is **cinematic** — strong light, deep shadow, high contrast, caught motion. Intimacy and cinema are not opposites. The closest analogy is a still from a film where the camera happened to catch something the character wasn't performing.

Three constraints that keep PROVEN out of the lab:

1. **The photograph should feel like a secret.** Not something taken — something witnessed. The viewer's first instinct should be "I wasn't supposed to see this," not "someone is being examined."
2. **The light should have opinion.** It rakes across a cheekbone, puts half the face in shadow, catches one arm and loses the other. Even though the source is cool and precise, the effect is dramatic — because the skin and materials carry all the warmth against that cool, directional structure.
3. **The image should make you want to touch your own face.** Not out of concern. Out of recognition. The viewer sees texture they know from their own mirror. That mirror-recognition is the brand.

If a PROVEN skin image reads as "data collection," it failed. If it reads as "a quiet, cinematic moment with someone's real face," it succeeded — and the data overlay, when added, arrives as insight, not measurement.

## Color Contract

Rose-warm axis: linen (#FAF6F2) → sand (#E6DED4) → rose (#DDBFB0). Pink-beige warmth. Never amber, golden, orange. This axis governs the backdrop, the grade direction, and the world around the skin — not the skin itself. Skin tones are protected by the tolerance table below.

**Banned:** "golden hour." Always "diffused north-facing window."

### Base Grade

| Parameter | Direction |
|---|---|
| Orange saturation | –15 to –20% |
| Warmth/tint | Toward magenta, away from yellow |
| Highlights | Clean neutral (no golden cast) |
| Shadows | Cool neutral, not bronze |
| Midtones | Rose-warm |

### Skin-Tone-Specific Tolerances

The base grade is a starting point, not a uniform filter. Darker skin retains depth and radiance — the grade should be invisible, never imposed.

| Tone Range | Orange Sat Reduction | Shadow Treatment | Midtone Warmth | Gate Test |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Fair / light** | Full –15 to –20% | Cool neutral | Rose-warm | Skin on linen (#FAF6F2) — same family |
| **Light-medium** | Full –15 to –20% | Cool neutral | Rose-warm | Skin on linen — same family |
| **Medium / olive** | –10 to –15% (lighter touch) | Neutral — preserve olive undertone | Warm but allow natural yellow-green if present | Skin on sand (#E6DED4) — harmonious, not identical |
| **Medium-deep / warm** | –8 to –12% (preserve richness) | Warm neutral — never desaturate to gray | Allow natural warmth — pull amber only, keep depth | Skin on sand or rose (#DDBFB0) — the skin should be the richest thing in the frame |
| **Deep / dark** | –5 to –10% (minimal — protect radiance) | Preserve shadow depth — never flatten or gray out | The skin's own warmth leads. Remove only orange *cast*, not inherent warmth. | Skin should glow against linen with full dimensional depth. If it looks washed, gray, or flat — the grade went too far. |

**The universal rule:** If the grade is *noticeable* — if it looks like a filter was applied — it went too far. The grade is invisible correction, not an aesthetic. Truth observed, not truth imposed.

**5000–5200K.** Diffused north-facing window. NOT golden hour.

## The Anchor

### Primary: Cheek Terrain (Activated Terrain)

This is PROVEN's single-frame signature. If the brand had one photograph, this is it.

**The shot:**
- Zone: mid-cheek to jawline
- Expression: **Variant A (The Lift)** — bright, confident, chin slightly up — or **Variant B (The Almost-Smile)** — one corner barely lifted, cheek muscles just engaging
- The micro-expression activates the cheek terrain: pore texture becomes visible as muscle engagement shifts the surface, the texture gradient from smooth orbital bone to textured jaw comes alive
- Lens: **85–105mm equivalent feel.** Close enough for pore-level detail, far enough to feel like observation rather than intrusion.
- Framing: skin fills 70–90% of frame. Nose tip at one edge, jaw at bottom. Ear out.
- DOF: one sharp band across the cheek — pores razor-detailed in that band, gentle falloff in front and behind. The falloff is what makes it intimate rather than clinical.
- Camera: 20° off-axis, slightly below eye level
- Negative space: 20–30% quiet zone, upper-left or upper-right. Even when no overlay is used, the quiet zone stays — it's compositional breathing room, not just a UI placeholder.

**All three myth details are mandatory in the hero:**
1. The lift, the almost-smile, the drift, or the glint (Activated Terrain)
2. The one-band depth of field (pores sharp, falloff beyond — intimacy, not microscopy)
3. The quiet negative space (the breath in the frame)

### Secondary: The 6-Angle Atlas

The full set. When the user asks for skin photography, generate all 6. The atlas documents everything the algorithm reads — but the Cheek Terrain is always the anchor, the others orbit it.

| # | Angle | Zone | Framing | Best Variant | Why |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Cheek Terrain** | Mid-cheek to jaw | **Face context** — facing camera, 85mm, 6in | A or B | Expression activates cheek surface |
| 2 | **Profile** | Full side — temple to jaw | **Profile** — 45–60°, 85mm, 8in | C (The Drift) | Calm, unaware of being observed — the algorithm watching |
| 3 | **Jawline Ridge** | Jaw angle to chin | **Zone crop** — 100mm, 4in | None | Below expressive muscles — pure terrain |
| 4 | **Under-Eye Basin** | Lower lid to upper cheek | **Zone crop** — 100mm, 4in | B (hint only) | Almost-smile barely lifts orbicularis |
| 5 | **Forehead Expanse** | Hairline to brow | **Zone crop** — 100mm, 4in | None | Terrain only — the sun-damage archive |
| 6 | **Nasolabial Valley** | Nose-side to mouth | **Face context** — facing camera, 85mm, 6in | D | Expression creates the fold — fold IS data |

**Angle specs — three framing modes:**

The 6-angle atlas uses three framing modes: **zone crop** (skin as terrain, camera 4 inches from surface, 100mm macro), **face context** (partial face facing camera, expression-activated), and **profile** (45–60° or full profile, the algorithm observing from the side).

**Face context (2 of 6) — facing camera, partial face:**

Camera direction: **subject faces camera.** This eliminates random off-axis inconsistency. The crop is what makes it partial, not the angle.

**1. Cheek Terrain** — 85mm, 6 inches from skin. Subject faces camera. Cropped tight to one side — one eye partially visible at top, nose at one side, jaw at bottom. The cheek is the subject. The anchor shot — generates first, used as reference for all others.

**6. Nasolabial Valley** — 85mm, 6 inches from skin. Subject faces camera. Cropped tight to nose-to-mouth — nostril wing at one edge, mouth corner at bottom. The nasolabial fold runs through the frame. Expression activates the fold.

**Profile (1 of 6) — the algorithm observing from the side:**

**2. Profile / Three-Quarter** — 85mm, 8 inches from skin. Subject at 45–60° or full profile. One side of the face, jawline to temple visible, far eye hidden. The most cinematic angle — the algorithm watching someone who doesn't know they're being read. Expression: **The Drift** — calm, eyes open and soft, no agenda.

**Zone crops (3 of 6) — skin as landscape:**

**3. Jawline Ridge** — 100mm macro, 4 inches from skin. Chin at one edge, jaw angle at the other, neck beginning at bottom. Like a ridgeline. No expression needed. The texture transition zone.

**4. Under-Eye Basin** — 100mm macro, 4 inches from skin. Lower lash line at top, cheekbone at bottom. The thinnest skin on the face. One eye barely visible at top edge. The data-rich canary.

**5. Forehead Expanse** — 100mm macro, 4 inches from skin. Hairline at top, eyebrow at bottom edge. Almost planetary. No expression needed. The sun-damage archive.

## Overlay Rule: 70 / 30

The skin is the anchor. The interface is not.

- **70% of skin assets: Pure Terrain.** No glass panels, no chips, no data. Just the skin, the expression, the light, the quiet zone breathing. The image stands alone.
- **30% of skin assets: Instrumented Terrain.** Glass stat panel in the quiet zone, 1–2 insight chips. The data arrives as insight, not measurement — because 70% of the time the viewer has already seen the skin without annotation.

When generating a 6-angle set, default: 4 pure + 2 instrumented (typically Cheek Terrain + one data-rich angle like Under-Eye Basin or Nasolabial Valley get the overlays).

The quiet zone exists in every composition regardless — it's the breath in the frame, not just a UI reservation.

## Expression Variants

#### A — "The Lift"
Positive, energized, confident. Eyes bright, chin slightly up, the face of someone who just got good news or caught their own reflection and liked what they saw.

```
bright, energized expression — eyes open and alive, chin tilted slightly up,
the face of someone who just caught their reflection and thought "yes,"
lips naturally together or barely parted with the hint of a grin,
brow relaxed and open, no tension — confidence without effort,
micro-expression: positive, self-assured, radiant, fully human
```

#### B — "The Almost-Smile"
One corner barely lifted. The half-second before a real smile.

```
barely-there asymmetric almost-smile —
one corner of the mouth just beginning to lift,
cheek muscles softly engaged but not bunched,
eyes soft, unfocused or gazing slightly down,
not a smile — the warmth before a smile,
micro-expression: private warmth, unperformed
```

#### C — "The Drift"
Calm, chill, relaxed. No agenda. The face between moments — not performing rest, just being in it.

```
calm, completely relaxed expression — no agenda on the face,
eyes open and soft, looking at nothing in particular, unfocused gentle gaze,
jaw loose, lips in natural rest, breath slow,
the face between moments — after coffee, before the day starts,
not performing relaxation — just being in it,
micro-expression: serene, unhurried, fully human,
eyes are OPEN — not closed, not half-closed, not squinting
```

#### D — "The Glint"
A little naughty, playful, witty. The face of someone who knows something you don't — and is enjoying it.

```
playful, slightly mischievous expression —
eyes bright with a knowing look, one eyebrow barely lifted,
the corner of the mouth curling with a secret,
the face of someone who just thought of something clever and is deciding whether to say it,
not a smirk — warmer than that, more conspiratorial than performative,
micro-expression: witty, cunning, delighted, fully human
```

## Prompt Templates

**Layer C from here forward.** The Feeling, the Color Contract, the Activated Terrain system, the expression variants — all Layer A and B. The templates below are Layer C — only pixel-driving language enters the generator. "The gaze observes" becomes "eyes soft, unfocused gentle gaze, the face between moments." Use the hub's translation table.

Three prompt modes matching the three framing modes. **Never list what should be excluded from frame** — exclusion lists cause sectioned compositions. Describe what the camera SEES using physical crop language.

### Mode 1: Face Context (Cheek, Nasolabial) — subject faces camera

Subject faces the camera directly. The crop makes it partial, not the angle. One eye max. Never both eyes.

```

### Mode 2: Profile (Angle 2) — the algorithm observing

45–60° or full profile. The most cinematic angle — one side of the face visible, far eye hidden. The algorithm watching someone who doesn't know they're being read.

```
Close-up profile photograph, 85mm f/2.8, subject at [45° / 60° / full profile], camera 8 inches away,
[EXPRESSION — The Drift: calm, eyes open and soft, unfocused gentle gaze, no agenda, eyes are OPEN],
real person not a model — [same person description as Angle 1],

The word "portrait" never appears. These are macro photographs of a surface.

```
Macro photograph of human [zone] skin, 100mm f/2.8, camera 4 inches from skin surface,
the [zone] fills the entire frame like [terrain metaphor] — [physical edge descriptions],
[expression if relevant, or omit for pure terrain zones],
same person as reference image — [key continuity cues: skin tone, hair color at hairline, sun spots],
bare skin, no makeup, [zone-specific texture: pores, veins, lines, shine],
warm raking light from the left at 45° — single perceived source, shadow side falls to soft darkness, 5000K, plain flat cream (#FAF6F2) backdrop,
razor-sharp focus on the skin surface, 4:5
```

**The split rule:** 2 face context (facing camera) + 1 profile + 3 zone crops per 6-angle set.

## Making People Look Real

Skin imperfections alone don't make someone look real. AI models will add pores to a face that still has a perfect nose, perfect jawline, symmetrical features, and salon hair. The *whole person* needs to read as someone you'd actually know.

*Features — break the symmetry:*
- Nose: "slightly wide nose," "a nose with character," "prominent nose bridge," "soft rounded nose tip"
- Face shape: "round face," "long face," "wide-set eyes," "close-set eyes," "strong brow ridge," "soft chin," "full cheeks"
- Teeth (if visible): "slightly crooked teeth," "gap between front teeth," "natural teeth"

*Body — break the mold:*
- "Full-figured," "broad shoulders," "soft jawline," "double chin visible at this angle," "thick neck"
- Don't describe body type as a negative — just be specific.

*Hair — break the salon:*
- "Frizzy hair," "graying at the temples," "thin hair pulled back in a scrunchie," "short practical haircut," "grown-out roots," "coarse curly hair," "wispy bangs"
- Avoid: "flowing," "lustrous," "silky," "tousled" (model-hair words)

*Age — commit to it:*
- If she's 45, she looks 45. Crow's feet, neck texture, smile lines that don't disappear at rest.
- "Looks young for her age" is a trap — it undoes the age you just specified.

*Vibe — give her a life:*
- "Looks like she runs a bookshop," "looks like your friend's mom who always hosts Thanksgiving," "looks like she just got off a bike"
- The vibe tells the model what *kind* of person, not how attractive she is.

*Banned words (trigger model-face):*
"beautiful," "stunning," "gorgeous," "radiant," "flawless," "luminous," "glowing," "striking," "elegant," "delicate," "refined," "sculpted," "chiseled," "high cheekbones," "full lips," "almond eyes," "porcelain"

**Expression lines (one per prompt — pick from variants A–D):**
```
A (The Lift):     confident, bright eyes, chin slightly up, hint of a grin — just caught her reflection and liked it
B (Almost-Smile): one corner of mouth barely lifting, eyes soft, the warmth before a smile
C (The Drift):    calm, eyes open and soft, unfocused gentle gaze, jaw loose, the face between moments — no agenda, eyes are OPEN
D (The Glint):    playful, one eyebrow barely raised, mouth curling with a secret she's deciding whether to share
```

**Zone emphasis (not zone isolation):**
```
Cheek:      camera angled toward mid-cheek, 20° off-axis, slightly below eye level
Temple:     camera angled toward temple, 20° off-axis, eye level
Jawline:    camera angled toward jaw, 20° off-axis, below eye level
Under-eye:  camera angled toward under-eye, 15° off-axis, eye level
Forehead:   camera angled toward forehead, 15° off-axis, above eye level
Nasolabial: camera angled toward nose-to-mouth fold, 20° off-axis, mouth level
```

## Workflow

1. **Always ask for preferences before generating:**
   - **Skin tone:** What tone? (e.g., "fair warm," "medium olive," "deep cool," or "full spectrum")
   - **Expression preference:** Any specific emotions they want? Default is A–D auto-assigned per atlas, but user can override.
   - If user doesn't specify, ask. Don't assume.
2. **Write a person description** with a distinguishing imperfection, specific features (nose, face shape, hair), and a vibe. Not a beauty descriptor.

**Planner Checkpoint — Know Who You're Looking At**

You've chosen the person and the expression. Before the camera gets close, confirm you know exactly what the light will find.

| Core slot | Decision |
|---|---|
| dominant_source | Single perceived source, directional, cool, 5000K. The light has opinion — it rakes, catches, puts areas in shadow. |
| warmth_carrier | The skin itself + cream (#FAF6F2) backdrop. Skin carries its own natural warmth. |
| camera_distance | Per-angle spec: face context = 85mm at 6 inches. Profile = 85mm at 8 inches. Zone crop = 100mm at 4 inches. |
| focus_hierarchy | One sharp band across the target zone. Gentle falloff in front and behind. The falloff is what makes it intimate rather than clinical. |
| chrome_presence | Absent. The camera's gaze is the responsive element. |
| hero_event | The micro-expression activating the skin terrain. The Activated Terrain IS the event. |

| SKIN slot | Decision |
|---|---|
| body_zone | Angle spec: cheek / profile / jawline / under-eye / forehead / nasolabial |
| pose_state | Expression variant: caught (A: Lift, B: Almost-Smile, D: Glint) or drift (C: The Drift) |
| skin_texture_priority | Always high. Pores visible, lines preserved, texture unretouched. |
| quiet_zone_location | 20–30% of frame, upper-left or upper-right. Compositional breathing room. |
| expression_variant | A / B / C / D — assigned per angle or user override |

All filled → proceed to step 3.

3. **Generate Angle 1 (Cheek, facing camera) first.** This is the anchor. Use it as image reference for remaining angles if the tool supports it.
4. **Generate remaining 5 angles** — same person description, different framing mode + zone + expression. Keep each prompt under 10 lines.
5. **Apply tone-specific grade.**
6. **Consistency check:** all 6 side by side. Same person, same light, same background. Reject and re-gen any outlier.

## Post-Production

- **Bare skin only.** No makeup, no foundation, no lip color. If a generated image shows visible cosmetics, reject and re-roll.
- **Crop zones in post.** The prompt generates a close-up portrait with zone emphasis. Crop tighter in post to isolate the zone for atlas layout.
- Preserve all texture. Pores, fine lines, topography.
- **Preserve expression-activated lines.** The fold from an almost-smile stays. The expression IS the terrain.
- Smooth color only. No frequency separation.
- Zoom test: pores visible at 100%, person distinguishable.
- **Real-person check:** Does she look like someone you'd meet in real life? If she looks like a model, reject.

**Post-production grade:**
```
Grade per skin-tone tolerance table.
[Fair/light]: orange sat –15 to –20%, cool shadows, rose midtones, linen gate.
[Medium/olive]: orange sat –10 to –15%, preserve olive, sand gate.
[Medium-deep]: orange sat –8 to –12%, preserve depth, sand/rose gate.
[Deep/dark]: orange sat –5 to –10%, protect radiance, glow-against-linen gate.
If the grade is noticeable, it went too far.
```

**For instrumented terrain (30% of set), add:**
```
Glass stat panel in quiet zone:
warm glass (rgba(250,246,242,0.65)),
large number (e.g., "62%"), 1-line descriptor
(e.g., "HYDRATION" in teal-dark #4A6D7C),
the data arrives as insight, not measurement.
Optional: 1 insight chip (pill-shaped, sky #BCD4E2 at 70%).
```

## Fix Recipes

When an image fails specific criteria, **append these lines to the original prompt** and regenerate. Don't rewrite — append.

#### FIX: Model Face (fails #1 or #10)
```
this person is NOT a model — asymmetric features, imperfect bone structure,
a face with character not beauty, the kind of person you'd see at a grocery store,
not conventionally attractive, not ugly — just a real human face
```

#### FIX: Flat / Worried Expression (fails #2)
```
[Re-paste the expression line as the FIRST line of the prompt, before everything else.
Then add at the END:]
the expression is the most important thing in this image —
she is [confident and bright / quietly warm / completely relaxed / playfully amused],
this is NOT a neutral expression, NOT worried, NOT tired, NOT blank
```

#### FIX: Makeup Present (fails #3)
```
absolutely no makeup of any kind — no foundation, no concealer, no lip tint,
no even skin coverage, skin tone varies naturally across the face,
lips are their natural color with natural dryness and texture,
this is a bare face first thing in the morning
```

#### FIX: AI-Smooth Skin (fails #4)
```
extreme skin detail — every pore visible, fine lines visible even in soft focus,
natural skin unevenness, slight redness in some areas, texture varies across zones,
this is real human skin photographed at 8 inches, NOT retouched, NOT smoothed,
documentary photography not beauty photography
```

#### FIX: Fabric / Room Background (fails #5)
```
shot in a photo studio against seamless paper backdrop,
completely flat uniform solid off-white background with zero texture,
no fabric, no linen, no curtain, no wall, no room visible,
studio paper roll backdrop, perfectly smooth and featureless
```

#### FIX: Golden / Amber Cast (fails #6)
```
NOT golden hour — cool-warm directional light, 5000K color temperature,
no amber cast, no golden glow, no warm haze,
clean neutral highlights with slight rose-pink warmth in midtones,
the light is clear and bright, not filtered or hazy
```

#### FIX: Consistency Drift (fails #9, angles 2–6)
```
[Use the Angle 1 output as a reference image if the tool supports it.
If text-only, copy the person description from Angle 1 verbatim and add:]
SAME person as previous image — identical bone structure, identical skin,
identical hair, identical background, identical lighting direction and quality,
only the camera angle and expression have changed
```

#### FIX: Eyes Closed / Squinting (fails #2 on calm expressions)
```
eyes are fully OPEN — not closed, not half-closed, not squinting, not sleepy,
soft relaxed gaze looking at nothing in particular but eyes are clearly open,
you can see the iris and pupil, eyelids relaxed but not drooping,
this person is awake, calm, and looking at something with soft focus
```

## AI Generation Guard Rails

Image models have predictable failure modes with close-up skin photography. These are the traps and how to avoid them:

**Model face — the #1 problem.** AI models default to casting-agency beauty. Adding "visible pores" to this face just makes a model with texture. The fix is to specify the *whole person* — nose shape, face shape, hair texture, body type, age commitment. See "Making People Look Real" above.

**Makeup.** Models default to foundation, lip color, concealer. Every prompt needs "bare skin, no makeup, no cosmetics." If the output shows even coverage — reject.

**Worry face.** Words like "tension," "concentration," "checking," "examining" produce furrowed brows. Use positive/neutral emotion words.

**Sectioned compositions.** Exclusion lists cause split-screen or collaged layouts. **Never list what should be excluded from frame.** For zone crops, use "Macro photograph of human [zone] skin" — drop "portrait." Describe what the camera sees using physical edges.

**Full-face gravitational pull.** Even with zone emphasis, models default to showing a full face with both eyes. The brand rule: never a full face, never both eyes visible.

**Glamour drift.** Even with "no makeup," models produce beauty-ad skin. Counter with specific imperfections.

**Set inconsistency.** Six separate prompts = six different people. Use image references when possible. Repeat person descriptions verbatim.

**Prompt length.** Under 10 lines. Every additional line gives the model another instruction to misinterpret.

**Expression gets buried by "real person."** When "real person, not a model" comes first and the expression is at line 5+, the model commits to "documentary" and renders it as tired/flat. **Fix: put the expression line at line 2.**

**Material names become textures.** "Solid linen background" → model renders linen *fabric*. Use "seamless paper backdrop, flat solid off-white."

**Head-and-shoulders default.** "Close-up portrait" produces head-and-shoulders, not extreme close-up. Use "extreme close-up" + physical distance specs.

**Eyes-closed trap.** "Half-closed," "heavy-lidded," "drowsy" all produce closed eyes. Always say **"eyes open and soft"** with reinforcement.

## Diversity

Real people, not models. Skin conditions included: acne, hyperpigmentation, rosacea, texture, sun damage, scarring. Age 25–60+. Full spectrum of skin tones. Equal lighting care. **Tone-appropriate grade — not a uniform filter.** Every person should look like someone you'd actually know. If the set looks like a casting call, it failed.

## What This Reference Refuses

- Amber/golden tones. "Golden hour."
- Ungraded outputs or uniform grade across all skin tones.
- Dead-eyed, blank, or mannequin expressions. Big open-mouth smiles. Direct stare into camera.
- **Makeup, foundation, cosmetics of any kind.**
- **Worry, frustration, concern, or self-consciousness** — every expression is positive or neutral-warm.
- **Model-pretty faces** — PROVEN faces look like someone you'd meet at a farmer's market.
- **Zone exclusion lists in prompts.**
- **Prompts over 10 lines.**
- Overlays on more than 30% of a set.
- Clinical framing.
- **Full face with both eyes visible** — never.
- **The word "portrait" in zone crop prompts.**
- **"Face fills the entire frame"** — banned (causes full-face outputs).
- Over-retouched skin or smoothed expression lines.
- Generating a single angle when user asked for "skin photography" — default is all 6.
- Stock photography.
- **AI-smooth skin** — if pores aren't visible, it's not PROVEN.
- **Environmental backgrounds** — solid color only. Use "seamless paper backdrop."
- **Tired, flat, or documentary-exhausted expressions.**
- **Beauty-ad language in prompts.**
